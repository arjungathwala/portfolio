PIC.ME

This project in particular is very close to my heart. My friend who is persuing a Ph.D. at UCSB resurned from a small village in Peru after working with autistic children with speech impairment. She realized that the current methods in place to assist these children are extremely obsolete and ineffective. Together, we came up with the idea for PIC.ME.

I developed this research-based, personalized Android application that helps autistic children with speech impairment overcome vocal challenges through a special use of media. It consists of multiple folders that consist of personalized information, such as kids' family members' pictures, facorite foods, drinks. The media captured by camera authentication is backed onto Google Firebase storage and can be retrieved.The app also consists of vocal assistance that teaches kids how to begin and end sentences. I am constantly adding features to the app as I go forward. We were one of 27 teams out of 438 competing teams to win UC Berkeley Big Ideas Competition for this project.

COVID Prediction Model

Computer Science is my greatest passion ever since I came to college. However, Mathematics was my first love. From elegant Discrete Math proofs to vast real world Calculus applications, Math always fascinated me. I decided to use this strength of Mathematics to create my first predictive model by solving set of differential equations.

My main objective with this project was to help my community understand how important social distancing is to limit the spread of COVID-19 Pandemic. I used the variable contact ratio to quantify how important social distancing is. During the quarter I made this project, I was taking a Differential Equations course and a Multivariable Calculus course. I turned my set of ordinary differential equations into a set of partial differential equations which depended on time and space. The model was a spinoff from the basic SIR pandemic model with space variable added. Now, with this added variable, pandemic could be viewed as spreading as a wave accross the population. The reason I decided to add space variable is because I feel like space is the most important variable deciding the spread of a pandemic like COVID-19 that is highly contagious. The project was recognized as the best project of the course.

Star Wars BB-8 Robot

This project is where the kid inside me was able to express himself. Growing up I was a huge Star Wars fan and found all Star Wars droid really cool. I worked on this projects as my first real team project. I enjoyed leading my team of three people to build this really cool robot; it would move around like a ball but its head remains upright on top by use of magnetic force.

I developed a simple android application that sends characters via Bluetooth every time a button is pressed. The Bluetooth module receives the data while the Arduino interprets and processes these data. The Arduino sends signals to the Motor Driver shield to give a go signal for the switching of the motors. We successfully built a functional BB-8 robot that could move around and talk using pre-recorded notes. My greates lesson while working on this project was that not everything goes as planned while working on a team project. I learned how to work with other engineers and manage them in cases I have to lead the project, as I had to do with this project. We won the prize for most innovative and creative project from UCSB ECE department for our work on this project.
